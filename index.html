Furniture Exchange Platform

Research-led requirements for a shared marketplace that streamlines how dorm residents buy and sell furniture

00 Furniture Exchange Platform

Requirements Gathering Methods & Data Collection

In order to collect the data necessary for thorough analysis, two complementary methods were used, which most effectively reflect public opinion regarding the question of creating a platform for the purchase and sale of furniture: semi-structured interviews taken with residents of several dormitories of Leipzig (Lumis, Basecamp, The Hood, SmartWings), and questionnaires of the residents of Lumis themselves, who provided different views on the question, based on their own experience of purchasing or selling things in different dormitories and the problems that arose as a result of this. These methods made it possible to deepen the given study with diverse, flexible data from various sources and to identify non-trivial behavioral patterns and motivations.

The interview protocol was designed with the goal of systematic collection of data from 8-10 residents of various dormitories with the deliberate selection of 2-3 examples from each identified category of persons: urgent need to purchase a product, exploratory viewing of purchase-sale offers, extremely limited means for purchasing a product, occasional sellers. This selection was made with the goal of covering the widest possible range of user experience and motivation.

Each interview lasts 15-20 minutes and takes place in a question-answer format from general topics to specific ones. In the beginning, taking about 2 minutes, the goals of the research are explained, its confidentiality is explained, and permission is asked for recording the conversation for further transcript and analysis. In the course of the conversation, the interview could deviate from the structure or from the initially set tasks; fragments of the recording in which this happens were not used in the transcript and review. The transcription of the conversation was carried out with the help of a program created by the author of this research, written in the programming language Python with built-in libraries, which ensures accurate translation from oral form to written without loss of information and words of the speaker.

01 Requirements Gathering Methods & Data Collection

Two complementary research streams captured the full range of resident experiences across Lumis, Basecamp, The Hood, and SmartWings. Semi-structured interviews provided rich storytelling on daily pain points, while targeted questionnaires quantified recurring frustrations and desired improvements.

Method 1: Semi-Structured Interviews

Eight to ten residents were selected deliberately to represent urgent buyers, exploratory browsers, students with limited means, and occasional sellers, ensuring wide coverage of motivations and expectations.

  • 15-20 minute conversations moved from broad context to specific transactions and frustrations.
  • Each participant granted recorded consent so the interview could be transcribed for deeper analysis.
  • A Python-based transcription tool created by the researcher delivered accurate, lossless text for review.

Method 2: Online Questionnaire

A structured survey of Lumis residents quantified how frequently exchanges succeed, which budget bands are most sensitive, and how existing WhatsApp workflows fall short.

  • Seven respondents shared tenure, residency status, and average spending on non-essential items.
  • Questions probed satisfaction scores, missed deals, and appetite for a dedicated platform.
  • Responses reinforced the need for safe, structured workflows that scale beyond a single dormitory.

Combining flexible interviews with quantitative questionnaires surfaced non-trivial behavioral patterns, confirmed recurring pain points, and informed the detailed requirements captured below.

02 Method 1 Interview Protocol

Below, in order to avoid repetitions in the future, a general protocol for conducting the interview is presented.

Introduction (2 minutes)

"Thank you for participating. This interview will take about 20-25 minutes. We're researching how Lumis residents buy and sell items, particularly through the WhatsApp group. Your responses are confidential and will help design a better solution. May I record this for analysis purposes? [Answer]"

Context (3-4 minutes)

"How long have you lived in Lumis? [Answer]. Are you an exchange student or regular student? [Answer]. What's your monthly budget for non-essential items? [Answer]."

Current Situation (8-10 minutes)

"Have you ever bought or sold items through the Lumis WhatsApp group? [Answer]. Walk me through the last time you tried to buy or sell something in the group. What happened? [Answer]. What was frustrating about that experience? [Answer]. How long did it take from posting to completing the transaction (or giving up)? [Answer]. Have you ever missed a good deal because you couldn't find the listing again? Tell me about it. [Answer]. How do you feel when you see buying or selling messages in the main WhatsApp group? [Answer]."

Needs and Preferences (6-8 minutes)

"What would make buying or selling items easier for you? [Answer]. How important is it that items are from Lumis residents only versus the broader Leipzig area? [Answer]. Would you pay a small fee (for example, 1 euro) to list an item if it meant better visibility? Why or why not? [Answer]. What information do you need to see in a listing before contacting a seller? [Answer]. How do you prefer to communicate with buyers or sellers? (WhatsApp, in-app chat, email?) [Answer]. What concerns do you have about buying used items from fellow residents? [Answer]."

Comparative Experience (3-4 minutes)

"Have you used platforms like eBay, Vinted, or Facebook Marketplace? What did you like or dislike? [Answer]. What features from those platforms would be useful for a Lumis-specific platform? [Answer]."

Conclusion (1 minute)

"Is there anything else about buying or selling in Lumis that we haven't discussed? [Answer]."

03 Interview Reports

Individual conversations with residents captured urgent needs, emotional cues, and concrete breakdowns in the current WhatsApp-based exchange process.

Pierre
First-year international student • 23 minutes
Pierre is a first-year student living in Lumis whose experience searching for everyday necessities in the WhatsApp group is overwhelmingly frustrating.

Summary

Pierre described how a request to buy a frying pan at 14:00 was buried by 15:00 under hundreds of unrelated messages. Reposting at later times produced the same result, forcing him to pay higher prices on external marketplaces like Amazon. He called out how a single, non-categorized chat blends multiple topics, so focused requests disappear almost immediately.

Observations

He displayed visible frustration while scrolling through the chat, mentioned urgency seven times, and stressed that rapid access to relevant listings is his highest priority.

Jose
Exchange student leaving soon • 26 minutes
Jose is preparing to leave Lumis in two months and needs reliable ways to sell belongings before his departure.

Summary

Jose's attempt to sell a bookshelf through the WhatsApp group received no responses; when he reposted, he was accused of spamming and blocked. A potential buyer demanded delivery across the city, which was impossible. Eventually he abandoned selling altogether and gave everything away for free, wishing for a simple app where a single listing could stay discoverable.

Observations

He expressed guilt over spamming, shared photographs of unsold items, and repeatedly showed time pressure tied to his leaving date, highlighting the urgency of an efficient selling channel.

Sandra
Third-year student with disposable income • 21 minutes
Sandra browses out of interest and aesthetics, making purchases based on visual appeal rather than necessity.

Summary

She recounted missing a 300 euro AirPods Max listing because it vanished within two hours. Keyword search produced too many irrelevant results, and the chat format failed to deliver a "showcase effect." She advocated for a visually rich platform similar to a social feed to encourage daily browsing.

Observations

Sandra lit up when discussing visual shopping experiences, grew irritated by WhatsApp's search limitations, and repeatedly linked purchasing decisions to aesthetics and photography.

Alexander
Working student with tight budget • 28 minutes
Alexander balances study with work and tracks spending carefully, requiring transparency and efficiency in second-hand transactions.

Summary

He labeled existing student trading platforms as slow and unreliable, noting a three-day wait for a microwave response and listings written without critical details. Price comparison is essential for him, and he argued that sorting and filtering tools are necessary to stay within budget.

Observations

Alexander referenced a spreadsheet of his expenses, showed visible tension when discussing overspending, and emphasized how hidden fees or unclear pricing immediately break trust.

04 Method 2 Online Questionnaire

Seven Lumis residents supplied structured responses on tenure, budget, success rates, and expectations for a dedicated exchange platform.

Respondent Profile

Four of the seven respondents have lived in Lumis for at least half a year, indicating meaningful exposure to existing WhatsApp marketplaces. Most rent their rooms long term, and five of seven spend no more than 200 euros monthly on non-essential items.

Only around 40 percent of their attempted exchanges in WhatsApp groups succeed, and the overall satisfaction score averages three out of five, revealing a split between mildly positive and clearly frustrated experiences.

Current Pain Points

Respondents highlighted missing structure and unreliable follow through: buyers cannot locate items quickly, sellers often fail to reply, and discount opportunities disappear in chat history without action. Communication breakdowns and organizational chaos were the most frequent complaints.

Platform Expectations

Students overwhelmingly desire a dedicated platform offering speed, safety, and structure. They prefer convenient search, a well-organized interface, and secure payments within the application. While most want the service to remain free, a small fee to cover maintenance is acceptable, and few require it to be limited to a single dormitory.

Adoption Signals

Every participant endorsed developing a standalone application or website and expressed willingness to help test it. The majority want a solution that can scale across Leipzig residences, mixing social network familiarity with reliable marketplace workflows.

05 Complete Requirements List

Based on the results of interviews and surveys, the following functional, non-functional, and usability requirements define the Furniture Exchange Platform.

F

Functional Requirements

F-01

Item Listing Management

  • The system shall allow users to create item listings with title, description, price, and photos.
  • Sellers shall be able to upload up to five photos per listing.
  • The system shall allow users to mark items as "sold" or "available."
  • Listings shall automatically be removed after thirty days or when marked as sold.
F-02

Search and Discovery

  • The system shall provide a search function by item name or an id.
  • Users shall be able to filter results by category and by price range.
  • Sorting options must include price.
  • A browsable feed of all available items shall be displayed.
F-03

User Communication

  • App messaging shall be provided between buyers and sellers.
  • Users shall also be allowed to initiate a new contact.
  • Seller contact information shall be shown only after the buyer expresses a specific interest.
F-04

User Management

  • Lumis accommodation shall have an email verification.
  • User profiles shall be maintained with contact information.
  • Users shall be able to view their own listing history.
F-05

Favorites and Saved Items

  • Users shall be allowed to save specific items to buy later.
  • The system must notify users when the price of favorite items changes.
F-06

Availability and Status

  • Item availability status shall be clearly displayed.
  • Sellers shall be allowed to mark items as "pending" during negotiations.
  • Sold items shall be hidden from main search results.
NFR

Non-Functional Requirements

NFR-01

Performance

  • The main feed shall load within two seconds on standard mobile internet.
  • Search results must appear within one second of query submission.
  • The system shall support up to 500 concurrent users.
NFR-02

Usability

  • New users must be able to create their first post within three minutes.
  • Users must be able to find a specific item using search within thirty seconds.
  • The system shall be accessible on mobile devices.
NFR-03

Reliability

  • The system must maintain 99 percent uptime during peak hours.
  • User data shall be backed up daily, and photos must be stored reliably without data loss.
NFR-04

Security

  • Only verified Lumis residents shall access the platform.
  • User contact information shall be protected until mutual interest is established.
  • The system must comply with GDPR data protection requirements.
NFR-05

Scalability

  • The system must be designed to scale to other accommodations in Leipzig.
  • The database shall handle up to 10,000 posts without performance degradation.
NFR-06

Cost

  • The platform must be free for buyers to use.
  • Listing fees, if any, shall not exceed 1 euro per item, and no transaction fees shall be charged on completed sales.
UX

User Experience Requirements

UX-01

Learnability

  • First-time users shall understand core functions without a tutorial.
  • The system shall provide tooltips for advanced features.
  • Error messages must be clear and actionable.
UX-02

Efficiency

  • Users shall complete a listing creation in a maximum of five clicks.
  • Search filters shall be accessible within two clicks from the home screen.
  • Frequent actions must be accessible from the home screen.
UX-03

Visual Design

  • The interface shall use a clean, modern design with high contrast.
  • Item photos must be prominent in listings.
  • Price shall be immediately visible on listing cards.
  • The color scheme shall be welcoming and trustworthy.
UX-04

Error Prevention

  • The system shall confirm before deleting listings.
  • Price inputs must be validated.
  • The system shall prevent duplicate listings within 24 hours.
UX-05

Satisfaction

  • The system must provide positive feedback after successful actions.
  • Empty states shall encourage and guide users to action.
  • The system must feel faster than scrolling through external messaging apps.
UX-06

Accessibility

  • Text shall meet WCAG AA contrast standards.
  • Interactive elements shall be a minimum of 50 by 50 pixels.
  • The system must be navigable using a keyboard only.

06 Conclusion

As a conclusion of the conducted survey among dormitory residents, both Lumis and others located in Leipzig, it can be noted that WhatsApp groups are not a safe and convenient tool for the purchase and sale of personal items, since they do not provide a centralized and understandable platform with a clear structure.

Every survey participant supported the idea of creating a separate application or website where exchange could be carried out safely, and also expressed willingness to participate in its development or testing within their available time. Despite the fact that monetization of the platform is not welcomed by the majority, the possibility of charging a small fee to cover technical maintenance expenses is allowed.

Students want to see a simple, structured, convenient, and safe application combining elements of a social network and an online store, which would provide efficient exchange of goods and multi-level communication between users.
